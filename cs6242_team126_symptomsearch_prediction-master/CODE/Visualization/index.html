Does Weather Impact Internet Search Behavior for Medical Symptoms?

Through this project, we want to explore and analyze the impact of different weather metrics like temperature, humidity, etc. on the U.S. populationâ€™s internet search behavior for various medical symptoms across counties.

We are using ARIMA (Auto Regressive Integrated Moving Average) for model training and analysis. Below metrics are used for evaluation:
RMSE (Root Mean Square Error) is the squared distance measure between the predicted numeric target and the actual numeric answer. A model with perfect predictions would have an RMSE of 0.
MAPE (Mean average percent error) expresses accuracy as a percentage. It is often useful for reporting because it uses generic percentage terms.

On this map, red counties  have lesser RMSE than green counties.  The lesser the RMSE value the better is the training model fit for the selected symptom for this county. Grey counties have insufficient data to model and map.

Select the symptom, and the training model to see and explore the results for different counties. Double click on the county will show the model comparison (using RMSE). It will also show the actual vs. predicted values for all models over the period of 9 weeks for the selected county and symptom.
